量子化学中使用的近似方法

发表于 2021-01-29 更新于 2021-02-04 分类于 理论学习

Chapter 5 of ‘量子化学_mooc_NJU’

大多数原子和分子的薛定谔方程无法精确求解，因此发展了一系列近似求解方法。

  1. Variational Method

  2. Linear Variational Principle and Secular Determinant

  3. Perturbation Theory and its Applications

1 Variational Method变分方法

Given a trial wave function试探波函数 $\varphi$,then:
$$E_\varphi = \frac{\int \varphi^* \hat{H} \varphi d\tau}{\int \varphi^* \varphi d\tau} \geq \varepsilon_0$$
$\varepsilon_0$: 体系的精确基态能量

If a trial function $\psi$ depends on some parameters, $\alpha,\beta,\gamma,…$, then:
$$E_\varphi(\alpha,\beta,\gamma,…) \geq \varepsilon_0$$
Now we can minimize $E_\varphi$ with respect to variational parameters to get the best possible ground-state energy.

Example: The ground state of a hydrogen atom

$$\hat{H} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m_e r^2} \frac{d}{dr}(r^2 \frac{d}{dr})-\frac{e^2}{4\pi \varepsilon_0 r}$$
Try a Gaussian function$\varphi(r)=e^{-ar^2}$ as a trial function, $\alpha$ being a variational parameter变分参数

$$E(\alpha)=\frac{3\hbar^2 \alpha}{2m_e}-\frac{e^2 \alpha^{1/2}}{\sqrt{2}\varepsilon_0 \pi^{3/2}}$$
求$E$的最小值:
$$\frac{dE(\alpha)}{d\alpha}=0 \Rightarrow \alpha=\frac{m_e^2 e^4}{18\pi^3 \varepsilon_0^2 \hbar^4}$$
$$E_{min}=-\frac{4}{3\pi}(\frac{m_e e^4}{16\pi^2 \varepsilon_0^2 \hbar^2}) > -\frac{1}{2}(\frac{m_e e^4}{16\pi^2 \varepsilon_0^2 \hbar^2})=\varepsilon_0$$

Exact wave function:
$$\psi_0(r,\theta,\varphi)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi}} a^{-3/2} e^{-r/a_0} \qquad \varepsilon_0=-\frac{1}{2}(\frac{m_e e^4}{16\pi^2 \varepsilon_0^2 \hbar^2})$$
Variational wave function:
$$\varphi_0(r,\theta,\varphi)=\frac{8}{3^{3/2}\pi} \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi a_0^3}} e^{-(8/9\pi)r^2/a_0^2} \qquad E_0=-\frac{4}{3\pi}(\frac{m_e e^4}{16\pi^2 \varepsilon_0^2 \hbar^2})$$

Example: Harmonic oscillator简谐振子

$$\hat{H}=-\frac{\hbar^2}{2\mu} \frac{d^2}{dx^2}+\frac{1}{2}kx^2$$
Try a function$\varphi=\cos \lambda x$ as a trial function, $\lambda$ being a variational parameter变分参数

$$E(\lambda)=\frac{\hbar^2 \lambda^2}{2\mu}+(\frac{\pi^2}{24}-\frac{1}{4})\frac{k}{\lambda^2}$$

$$E_{min}(\lambda)=(\frac{\pi^2}{3}-2)^{1/2} \frac{\hbar}{2} (\frac{k}{\mu})^{1/2} =(1.14)\frac{1}{2}\hbar\omega > \frac{1}{2}\hbar\omega=\varepsilon_0$$

Allocation of the Variational Method to Two-electron Problems

The Hamiltonian operator for $He$:

$$\hat{H}=(-\frac{\hbar^2}{2 m_e} \nabla_1^{2} -\frac{2 e^2}{4 \pi \varepsilon_0} \frac{1}{r_1}) + (-\frac{\hbar^2}{2 m_e} \nabla_2^{2}-\frac{2 e^2}{4 \pi \varepsilon_0} \frac{1}{r_2}) +\frac{e^2}{4 \pi \varepsilon_0} \cdot \frac{1}{r _{12}} $$
$$= \hat{H} _H(1)+\hat{H} _H(2)+\frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \varepsilon_0} \cdot \frac{1}{r _{12}}$$

量子力学基本假设antisymmetric： 对于该体系，交换两个电子的坐标，其波函数要取负值(反对称):$\Psi(x_1,x_2)=-\Psi(x_2,x_1)$

For $He$, its trial wave function must be antisymmetric about exchange of two electrons 1 and 2, $Z$ being a variational parameter变分参数:
$$\Psi_{0}(x_1, x_2)=\varphi_{0}(\vec{r}_1, \vec{r}_2) \Omega(\sigma_1, \sigma_2)$$

$$\varphi_0 (\vec{r_1}, \vec{r_2}) =\psi_{1s}(\vec{r_1}) \psi_{1s}(\vec{r_2}) \quad \Omega(\sigma_1, \sigma_2)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}[\alpha(\sigma_1) \beta(\sigma_2)-\beta(\sigma_1) \alpha(\sigma_2)]$$

$$\psi_{1s}(\vec{r}_j)=(\frac{Z^3}{\pi a_0^3})^{1/2} e^{Zr_j/a_0} \qquad j=1or2$$

Try the trial function:
$$E(Z)=\frac{m_e e^4}{16 \pi^2 \varepsilon_{0}^{2} \hbar^2}(Z^2-\frac{27}{8} Z)$$
$$\frac{dE}{dZ}=0 \Rightarrow Z_{min}=\frac{27}{16} \quad E_{min}=-(\frac{27}{16})^2=-2.8477$$
from experimental: $\varepsilon_0=-2.9033$
in units of $\frac{m_e e^4}{16 \pi^2 \varepsilon_{0}^{2} \hbar^2}$

有效核电荷$Z=\frac{27}{16} < 2$ : 两个电子之间的屏蔽作用

2 Linear Variational Principle线性变分方法 and Secular Determianant久期行列式

For a arbitrary任意的 wave function $| \phi >$, suppose it can be expanded as:
$$|\phi > =\sum\limits_{i=1}^N c_i |\psi > $$
where $|\psi >$ is a fixed set of $N$ basis functions.

Assume that the basis functions are real:
$$< \phi|\phi > = |\phi > =\sum\limits_{ij}c_i c_j < \psi_i|\psi_j >=\sum\limits_{ij}c_i c_j S_{ij}$$
$$< \phi|\hat{H}|\phi > = |\phi > =\sum\limits_{ij}c_i c_j < \psi_i|\hat{H}|\psi_j >=\sum\limits_{ij}c_i c_j H_{ij}$$
$H$ 和 $S$为对称矩阵：
$$ < \psi_i|\hat{H}|\psi_j > = H_{ij}= H_{ji} \qquad < \psi_i|\psi_j > = S_{ij}= S_{ji}$$

$$E=\frac{< \phi|\phi >}{< \phi|\hat{H}|\phi >}=\frac{\sum_{i=1}^N \sum_{j=1}^N c_i c_j H_{ij}}{\sum_{i=1}^N \sum_{j=1}^N c_i c_j S_{ij}}$$

To solve:
$$\sum_{i=1}^N \sum_{j=1}^N c_i c_j H_{ij}=E\sum_{i=1}^N \sum_{j=1}^N c_i c_j S_{ij}$$
之后两侧同时对$c_\kappa$求导

To get the minimum energy $E$, we must have:
$$\frac{\partial E}{\partial c_\kappa}=0 \quad (\kappa=1,2,…,N)$$

It is easy to prove the relationship:$\partial E / \partial c_\kappa=\delta_{i\kappa}$, ($\delta_{i\kappa}=0$ if $i=\kappa$, $\delta_{i\kappa}=1$ else)

Therefore:
$$\sum\limits_{i=1}^N c_i H_{i\kappa}+\sum\limits_{j=1}^N c_j H_{\kappa j}=E(\sum\limits_{i=1}^N c_i S_{i\kappa}+\sum\limits_{j=1}^N c_j S_{\kappa j})$$

With the relationship $H_{ij}= H_{ji}$, $S_{ij}= S_{ji}$, the above equation can be simplified as:
$$\sum\limits_{i=1}^N c_i H_{i\kappa}==E\sum\limits_{i=1}^N c_i S_{i\kappa}$$

Therefore:
$$\sum\limits_{i=1}^N (H_{i\kappa}-ES_{i\kappa})c_i=0, \quad (\kappa=1,2,…,N)$$

To have a nontrivial solution, the following Secular Determianant must be equal to $0$:
$$
\left|\begin{array}{cccc}
H_{11}-E S_{11} & H_{12}-E S_{12} & \cdots & H_{1 N}-E S_{1 N} \
H_{12}-E S_{12} & H_{22}-E S_{22} & \cdots & H_{2 N}-E S_{2 N} \
\vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \
H_{1 N}-E S_{1 N} & H_{2 N}-E S_{2 N} & \cdots & H_{N N}-E S_{N N}
\end{array}\right|=0
$$

By expanding this determinant, one obtains a Nth-order N阶 secular equation久期方程, which can be solved to get $N$ roots for $E$.
The smallest root corresponds to the ground-state energy.

Example

Determine the normalized trial function for a variational treatment of a particle in a box求解箱中粒子的试探波函数
$$\varphi=c_1x(1-x)+c_2x^2(1-x)^2 \quad (0 \leq x \leq 1)$$

SOLUTION:
The eogenvalues can be obtained by solving the equation below:
$$
\left|\begin{array}{ll}
H_{11}-E S_{11} & H_{12}-E S_{12} \
H_{12}-E S_{12} & H_{22}-E S_{22}
\end{array}\right|=0
$$

$$H_{11}=\int_0^1 x(1-x) \hat{H} x(1-x)dx=\int_0^1 x(1-x)[-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \frac{d^2}{dx^2}x(1-x)]dx=\frac{\hbar^2}{6m}$$
$$H_{12}=\frac{\hbar^2}{30m} \qquad H_{22}=\frac{\hbar^2}{105m}$$
$$S_{11}=\frac{1}{30} \qquad S_{12}=\frac{1}{140} \qquad S_{22}=\frac{1}{630}$$

The correspongding equation becomes:
$$E’^2-56E’+252=0 (E’=mE/\hbar^2)$$
$$E_{min}=4.93487\frac{\hbar^2}{m}$$

Back to the first equation:
$$
\left(\begin{array}{ll}
H_{11}-E_{\min } S_{11} & H_{12}-E_{\min } S_{12} \
H_{12}-E_{\min } S_{12} & H_{22}-E_{\min } S_{22}
\end{array}\right)\left(\begin{array}{c}
c_{1} \
c_{2}
\end{array}\right)=0
$$
These two equation are not independent of each other, so we solve only one, and get:
$$\frac{c_2}{c_1}=1.133$$
With the normalization condition:$\int \varphi^2(x)dx=1$ the normalized trial funtion corresponding to $E_{min} is$:
$$\varphi=4.404x(1-x)+4.990x^2(1-x)^2$$

The exact ground-state particle in a box wave function:
$$\psi_1(x)=2^{1/2}\sin \pi x$$

Another Situation

It is commom partice to use a trial function of the form:
$$\varphi=\sum\limits_{j=1}^N c_j f_j$$
The $f_j$ themselves may also contain variational parameters. $f_j$本身包含参数

An example of such a trial funcion for the hydrogen atom is:
$$\varphi=\sum\limits_{j=1}^N c_j e^{-\alpha_j r^2}$$
The $\mathbf{c_j}$ and the $\mathbf{\alpha_j}$ are treated as variational paramenters变分参数.

A number of optimization methods can be used to obtain the optimal $2N$ paramenters and the ground-state energy.

The exact value(-0.500) is approached as $N$ increase.

3 Perturbation Theory 微扰理论

Suppose we wish to solve the eigenvalue problem:
$$H|\psi_i\rangle =E_i|\psi_i\rangle $$
Let:
$$H=H_0+\nu$$
$H_0$: Zeroth-order Hamiltonian零级哈密顿
$\nu$: perturbation微扰项

Where wo know the eigenfunctions and eigenvalue of $H_0$:
$$H_0|\psi_i^{(0)}\rangle =E_i^{(0)}|\psi_i^{(0)}\rangle$$

How to select $\nu$：the perturbation $\nu$ should be small.

定义函数：
$$H(\lambda)=H_0+\lambda\nu$$
对其本征值$E_i(\lambda)$进行泰勒展开：
$$E_i(\lambda)=E_i^{(0)}+\lambda E_i^{(1)}+\lambda^2 E_i^{(2)}+…$$
其波函数也可以进行泰勒展开：
$$|\psi_i\rangle (\lambda)=|\psi_{i}^{(0)}\rangle+\lambda|\psi_{i}^{(1)}\rangle+\lambda^2|\psi_{i}^{(2)}\rangle+…$$

$E_i^{(n)}$: The $n$th-order correction
$\psi_i^{(n)}$: The $n$th-order wave function

Let $|i\rangle=|\psi_i^{(0)}\rangle$ and assume the intermediate normalization for $|\psi_i\rangle$中间归一化:
$$\langle i|\psi_i\rangle=\langle i|i\rangle + \lambda\langle i|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle+…=1$$
Thus:
$$\langle i|\psi_i^{(n)}\rangle =0 \qquad n=1,2,3,…$$

Substituting the expression of $H(\lambda)$,$E_i(\lambda)$ and $|\psi_i\rangle (\lambda)$ into the schrodinger equation:
$$(H_{0}+\lambda\nu)\left(|i\rangle+\lambda|\psi_{i}^{(1)}\rangle+\lambda^{2}|\psi_{i}^{(2)}\rangle+…\right)=\left(E_{i}^{(0)}+\lambda E_{i}^{(1)}+\lambda^{2} E_{i}^{(2)}+…\right)\left(|i\rangle+\lambda|\psi_{i}^{(1)}\rangle+…\right)$$

展开后，当且仅当$\lambda^n$各个次方项的系数相等才能使得方程成立：
$$n=0 \qquad H_0|i\rangle =E_i^{(0)}|i\rangle \qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\quad$$
$$n=1 \qquad H_0|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle+ \nu|i\rangle=E_i^{(0)}|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle+E_i^{(1)}|i\rangle$$
$$\quad\qquad\qquad \qquad n=2 \qquad H_0|\psi_i^{(2)}\rangle+ \nu|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle=E_i^{(0)}|\psi_i^{(2)}\rangle+E_i^{(1)}|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle+E_i^{(2)}|i\rangle$$
$$… \qquad\qquad … \qquad\qquad …$$

For a Hermitian operator:
$$\left\langle i|H_0|\psi_i^{(n)}\right\rangle=E_i^{(0)}\left\langle i|\psi_i^{(n)}\right\rangle=0 \qquad n=1,2,… \qquad \left(\langle f|\hat{A}|g\rangle=\langle \hat{A}f|g\rangle \right)$$

Multiplying each of these equations by $\langle i|$ and using the orthogonality relation:
$$E_i^{(0)}=\langle i|H_0|i\rangle$$
$$E_i^{(1)}=\langle i|\nu|i\rangle \quad$$
$$E_i^{(2)}=\langle i|\nu|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle$$
$$E_i^{(3)}=\langle i|\nu|\psi_i^{(2)}\rangle$$
$$… \qquad …$$

For example, let us start with the equation for $n=1$
$$H_0|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle+ \nu|i\rangle=E_i^{(0)}|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle+E_i^{(1)}|i\rangle$$

Multiplying this equations by $\langle i|$:
$$\langle i|H_0|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle+ \langle i|\nu|i\rangle=\langle i|E_i^{(0)}|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle+\langle i|E_i^{(1)}|i\rangle$$

We can derive:
$$E_i^{(1)}=\langle i|\nu|i\rangle=\langle \psi_i^{(0)}|\nu|\psi_i^{(0)} \rangle$$

By setting $\lambda=1$, we have:
$$H=H_0+\nu$$
$$E_i=E_i^{(0)}+\lambda E_i^{(1)}+\lambda^2 E_i^{(2)}+…$$
$$|\psi_i\rangle=|\psi_{i}^{(0)}\rangle+\lambda|\psi_{i}^{(1)}\rangle+\lambda^2|\psi_{i}^{(2)}\rangle+…$$

$$\psi_i=\psi_i^{(0)}+\psi_i^{(1)}+\psi_i^{(2)}+…$$
The first-order correction ($\psi_i^{(1)}$) is still quite substantial相当可观

First-order perturbation theory

(1) The first-order energy

$$E_i^{(0)}=\langle i|H_0|i\rangle=\langle \psi_i^{(0)}|H_0|\psi_i^{(0)}\rangle$$
$$E_i^{(1)}=\langle \psi_i^{(0)}|\nu|\psi_i^{(0)}\rangle$$
$$E_i=E_i^{(0)}+E_i^{(1)}=\langle \psi_i^{(0)}|H_0|\psi_i^{(0)}\rangle+\langle \psi_i^{(0)}|\nu|\psi_i^{(0)}\rangle=\langle \psi_i^{(0)}|H|\psi_i^{(0)}\rangle$$
The first-order energy can be obtained with the zero-order wave function

(2) The second-order energy

In the case $n=1$:
$$(E_i^{(0)}-H_0)|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle=(\nu-E_i^{(1)})|i\rangle$$
Multiplying both side by $\langle n |$:
$$\langle n |(E_i^{(0)}-H_0)|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle=\langle n |(\nu-E_i^{(1)})|i\rangle=\langle n |\nu|i\rangle \quad (n \neq i)$$

Expanding the first-order wave function is terms of the eigenfunction of $H_0$, one gets一级波函数展开为零级哈密顿的所有本征函数线性组合:
$$|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle={\sum\limits_m}’ |m\rangle C_m^{(1)} \quad (m \neq i)$$
So:
$$\langle n |(E_i^{(0)}-H_0){\sum\limits_m}’ |m\rangle C_m^{(1)} \quad (m \neq i)\rangle =(E_i^{(0)}-E_n^{(0)})C_n^{(1)}$$
$$C_n^{(1)}=\frac{\langle n |\nu|i\rangle}{E_i^{(0)}-E_n^{(0)}}$$

The first-order wave function:
$$|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle={\sum\limits_n}’ |n\rangle \frac{\langle n |\nu|i\rangle}{E_i^{(0)}-E_n^{(0)}} \quad (n \neq i)$$

$$E_i^{(2)}=\langle i|\nu|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle={\sum\limits_n}’ \langle i|\nu|n\rangle \frac{\langle n |\nu|i\rangle}{E_i^{(0)}-E_n^{(0)}}=\langle i|\nu|\psi_i^{(1)}\rangle={\sum\limits_n}’ \frac{\langle i|\nu|n\rangle \langle n |\nu|i\rangle}{E_i^{(0)}-E_n^{(0)}}$$

$$E_i=E_i^{(0)}+E_i^{(1)}+E_i^{(2)}=\left\langle \psi_i^{(0)} | H | \psi_i^{(0)} \right\rangle+ E_i^{(2)}$$

Application of Perturbation Theory to the Helium Atom

The Hamiltonian operator of $He$:
$$\hat{H} = \hat{H_H}(1) + \hat{H_H}(2) +\frac{e^2}{4\pi \varepsilon_0}\frac{1}{r_{12}} \qquad \hat{H_H} (i)=-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m_e}\nabla_i^2-\frac{2e^2}{4\pi \varepsilon_0}\frac{1}{r_i}\quad (i=1,2)$$

$$H _0 = \hat{H _H}(1) + \hat{H _H}(2) \qquad \nu=\frac{e^2}{4\pi \varepsilon_0}\frac{1}{r _{12}}$$

The zero-order wave function (of two zero-order Hamilton) are(空间部分×自旋部分):
$$\Psi_k^{(0)}=\varphi_k(\mathbf{r_1},\mathbf{r_2}) \Omega(\sigma_1,\sigma_2)$$
$$\Omega(\sigma_1,\sigma_2)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}[\alpha(\sigma_1)\beta(\sigma_2)-\beta(\sigma_1)\alpha(\sigma_2)]$$
$$\varphi_k(\mathbf{r_1},\mathbf{r_2})=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}[\psi_{nlm}(\mathbf{r_1})\psi_{n’l’m’}(\mathbf{r_2})+\psi_{nlm}(\mathbf{r_2})\psi_{n’l’m’}(\mathbf{r_1})]$$

The zero-order ground-state wave function and ground-state energy:
$$\Psi_k^{(0)}=\varphi_k(\mathbf{r_1},\mathbf{r_2}) \Omega(\sigma_1,\sigma_2)$$
$$\varphi_0(\mathbf{r_1},\mathbf{r_2})=\psi_{1s}(r_1,\theta_1,\varphi_1)\psi_{1s}(r_2,\theta_2,\varphi_2)$$
$$\psi_{1s}(\mathbf{r_j})=(\frac{Z^3}{\pi a_0^3})^{1/2} e^{-Zr_j/a_0} \qquad j=1,2$$

$$E_{0}^{(0)}=-\frac{Z^{2} m_{e} e^{4}}{32 \pi^{2} \varepsilon_{0}^{2} \hbar^{2}}-\frac{Z^{2} m_{e} e^{4}}{32 \pi^{2} \varepsilon_{0}^{2} \hbar^{2}} \quad(\mathrm{Z}=2)$$

The first-order energy correction is obtained as:
$$E_{0}^{(1)} =\left\langle\Psi_{0}^{(0)}|\nu| \Psi_{0}^{(0)}\right\rangle =\iint d \mathbf{r_1} d \mathbf{r_2} \psi_{1s}\left(\mathbf{r_1}\right) \psi_{1s}\left(\mathbf{r_2}\right) \frac{e^{2}}{4 \pi \varepsilon_{0} r_{12}} \psi_{1s}\left(\mathbf{r_1}\right) \psi_{1 s}\left(\mathbf{r_2}\right)=\frac{5 Z}{8}\left(\frac{m_{e} e^{4}}{16 \pi^{2} \varepsilon_{0}^{2} \hbar^{2}}\right)$$

With the first-order perturbation theory, the ground-state energy is (in the units of $\left(\frac{m_{e} e^{4}}{16 \pi^{2} \varepsilon_{0}^{2} \hbar^{2}}\right)$):
$$E_0 \approx E_0^{(0)}+E_0^{(1)}=-\frac{1}{2}Z^2-\frac{1}{2}Z^2+\frac{5}{8}Z \approx -2.8477 \quad (Z=2)$$

The second-order perturbation theory gives: $-2.910$
The higher-order calculation gives: $-2.9037$
The experimental result is: $-2.9033$

Application of Particle in a 1D Box

箱中粒子，势能不为0：
$$V(x)=\frac{V_0 x}{a} \qquad 0 \leq x \leq a$$

显然微扰项为：$\nu=V_0 x/a$

The zero-order functions and energies are:
$$\psi_n^{(0)}=(\frac{2}{a})^{1/2}\sin\frac{n\pi x}{a} \qquad E_n^{(0)}=\frac{n^2 h^2}{8ma^2}$$

The first-order energy correction is:
$$E_n^{(1)}=\int_0^a \psi_n^{(0)*} (\frac{V_0 x}{a}) \psi_n^{(0)}dx=\frac{V_0}{2}$$
Therefore, the first-order energy is:
$$E_n=\frac{n^2 h^2}{8ma^2}+\frac{V_0}{2} \qquad n=1,2,3,…$$
